Findings
1. No canonical schema contract: The protocol defines structure informally, but not a strict required-field set with machine-validatable constraints.
2. Weak provenance model: Claims are not required to include evidence type/source URI, so unverifiable assertions can look authoritative.
3. No version-negotiation signaling: Parsers cannot reliably discover compatibility rules (min_parser_version, deprecations, sunset dates).
4. Ambiguous recency semantics: last-modified exists, but no required timezone/format rule across all artifacts (agent-facts.html, llms.txt, spec metadata).
5. Insufficient abuse controls: No explicit policy for unverifiable superlatives, fake affiliations, or contradictory claims across pages.
Proposed Edits
1. Edit: Add normative "Core Required Fields" block
* Change text (ready to paste):
### Core Required Fields (Normative)
Every `/agent-facts.html` document MUST include exactly one value for:
`protocol_version`, `entity_legal_name`, `primary_url`, `last_modified_iso8601`, `jurisdiction`, `primary_contact`.
`last_modified_iso8601` MUST match RFC 3339 format (e.g., `2026-03-03T00:00:00Z`).
Missing required fields MUST cause validation failure.
* Why it helps: Enables deterministic parsing and validation.
* Compatibility impact: low
* Abuse risk impact: medium reduction
* One objective acceptance test: Validator fails if any required field is missing or duplicated.
2. Edit: Add claim provenance requirements
* Change text (ready to paste):
### Claim Provenance (Normative)
Each factual claim SHOULD include:
`claim_id`, `claim_text`, `source_type` (`official_record|first_party|third_party|self_asserted`), `source_url` (if available), `evidence_date`.
Claims marked `self_asserted` MUST be labeled as such in machine-readable output.
* Why it helps: Separates verified facts from self-asserted statements.
* Compatibility impact: low
* Abuse risk impact: high reduction
* One objective acceptance test: Any claim with source_type=self_asserted is emitted with explicit self-asserted label.
3. Edit: Add compatibility and deprecation metadata
* Change text (ready to paste):
### Compatibility Metadata (Normative)
Documents MUST declare:
`protocol_version`, `min_parser_version`.
Documents MAY declare:
`deprecated_fields` (array), `sunset_date` (RFC 3339 date-time).
Parsers MUST ignore unknown fields and MUST NOT fail on additive extensions.
* Why it helps: Stabilizes upgrades and prevents parser breakage.
* Compatibility impact: none
* Abuse risk impact: low reduction
* One objective acceptance test: Parser for v2.1 successfully parses document containing unknown additive fields.
4. Edit: Add cross-file consistency rules
* Change text (ready to paste):
### Cross-File Consistency (Normative)
`protocol_version` and `last_modified_iso8601` MUST match across:
`/agent-facts.html`, `/llms.txt`, and canonical spec metadata.
If values differ, consumers MUST treat the newest RFC 3339 timestamp as authoritative and log mismatch.
* Why it helps: Reduces stale or conflicting signals.
* Compatibility impact: low
* Abuse risk impact: medium reduction
* One objective acceptance test: CI fails when protocol_version differs between agent-facts.html and llms.txt.
5. Edit: Add anti-spam policy
* Change text (ready to paste):
### Abuse and Integrity Policy (Normative)
The following MUST NOT appear without third-party evidence URL:
- superlative claims (`#1`, `best`, `most trusted`)
- affiliations/certifications
- regulatory/compliance assertions
Unverifiable marketing claims MUST be excluded from machine-readable fact blocks.
* Why it helps: Limits manipulation of AI retrieval.
* Compatibility impact: low
* Abuse risk impact: high reduction
* One objective acceptance test: Lint rule flags banned claim classes lacking source_type=third_party and source_url.
